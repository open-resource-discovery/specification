# Governance

The Open Resource Discovery (ORD) project is governed by the **ORD Steering Committee** under the [NeoNephos Foundation](https://neonephos.org/projects/open-resource-discovery-ord/), a [Linux Foundation Europe](https://linuxfoundation.eu) project.

The authoritative governance materials — committee composition, decision-making process, charter, meeting cadence, and contribution policies — are maintained in the dedicated steering repository:

> **[github.com/open-resource-discovery/steering](https://github.com/open-resource-discovery/steering)**

This document summarizes how that governance interacts with the Community Specification License process for the specification developed in this repository.

## 1. Roles

- **Steering Committee** — Strategic direction, scope, and approval of new specification versions. See the [steering repository](https://github.com/open-resource-discovery/steering) for current membership.
- **Maintainers** — Day-to-day technical stewardship of the specification, review of pull requests, and consensus-building on technical proposals. The current core contributors are listed in the [README acknowledgements](./README.md#acknowledgements).
- **Contributors** — Anyone who has signed the Community Specification Contributor License Agreement and submitted a Contribution.
- **Participants** — All Contributors collectively, subject to the Community Specification License.

## 2. Decision Making

**2.1. Consensus-based.** Decisions on the specification are made by consensus among Maintainers, informed by input from Contributors and the Steering Committee. The agreement of all Participants is preferred but not required; Maintainers determine consensus in good faith based on the dominant view and the substance of objections.

**2.2. Appeals.** A decision may be appealed via a GitHub issue or pull request. Appeals are considered by the Maintainers in good faith. Unresolved appeals may be escalated to the Steering Committee.

## 3. Ways of Working

The project follows the consensus-based due process principles described in [Section 3 of the Community Specification Governance Policy 1.0](https://github.com/CommunitySpecification/Community_Specification/blob/main/05-governance.md): openness, lack of dominance, balance, coordination and harmonization, consideration of views and objections, and written procedures.

## 4. Specification Development Process

ORD follows the four stages defined by the Community Specification process: **Pre-Draft**, **Draft Specification**, **Working Group Approval**, and **Publication**. Releases are cut from `release/vX.Y.Z` branches and tagged in line with the [release process](./CHANGELOG.md).

Submissions of an Approved Specification to any external standards body (e.g., ISO/IEC, OASIS) require approval by the Steering Committee.

## 5. Code of Conduct

All participants are subject to the [LF Europe Code of Conduct](https://linuxfoundation.eu/policies/code-of-conduct). See [`Notices.md`](./Notices.md) for the designated points of contact.

## 6. Amendments

This governance document may be amended by approval of the Steering Committee, recorded in the [steering repository](https://github.com/open-resource-discovery/steering).
